✦ Features

Built for trust

Every feature is designed around one principle: your vote belongs to you, not to any server or administrator.

🔐
Wallet Identity

Every voter authenticates via their Phantom wallet. No accounts, no passwords — just cryptographically verified identity.

🚫
No Double Voting

PDA accounts on Solana enforce one vote per wallet. The blockchain itself — not us — prevents any duplicate submissions.

📡
On-Chain Storage

Polls, options, votes, and wallet addresses are all stored on Solana Devnet — no private server, no single point of failure.

⚡
Sub-second Finality

Solana processes thousands of transactions per second. Your vote is confirmed and immutable in under a second.

🔍
Public Verifiability

Anyone can independently verify any vote on Solana Explorer. Full transparency — no trust required.

🛡️
Admin-Proof

Smart contracts enforce the rules. Once a vote is cast, not even the poll creator can modify, remove, or override it.

✦ How It Works

Three steps to immutable democracy

Powered by Anchor Framework and Solana blockchain. Built in Rust for maximum security and performance.

01

Connect your Phantom Wallet

No registration. Your wallet address is your identity — cryptographically verified on every action you take.

02

Create or Find a Poll

Define your question and options. The create_poll instruction writes your poll directly to Solana blockchain.

03

Cast your Vote On-Chain

The vote instruction creates a PDA tied to your wallet — making double voting cryptographically impossible.

04

Watch Results in Real Time

Results are read directly from chain. No backend, no API — just raw blockchain truth, refreshed live.

✦ FAQ

Common questions

Everything you need to know about blockchain voting with LIBROS.

Voting on Solana Devnet is completely free. On mainnet, each vote costs a tiny fraction of SOL (typically less than $0.001) to cover the blockchain transaction fee. No hidden costs, no subscriptions.
Each vote creates a Program Derived Address (PDA) derived from your wallet address and the poll ID. This PDA can only be created once — if you try to vote again, Solana rejects the transaction because the PDA already exists. This is enforced by the blockchain, not by us.
No. Once a vote is submitted to the blockchain, it is immutable. The Anchor smart contract does not have any admin override function — not even the contract deployer can modify vote counts or remove votes.
You need Phantom Wallet — available as a browser extension for Chrome, Firefox, and Brave, and as a mobile app for iOS and Android. Make sure to switch it to Devnet in the settings when testing. Phantom is free to install and use.
Votes are pseudonymous — your wallet address is publicly recorded, but it doesn't directly reveal your real identity unless you've linked your wallet to your personal information elsewhere. The transparency trade-off is intentional: it enables public auditability.
Poll data remains permanently on the blockchain even after closing. All votes, wallet addresses, and final results are preserved forever and can be independently verified by anyone with the poll's address.
